GLORIA (Global Observation Research Initiative in Alpine Environments)

Contributing to Global Alpine Climate Change Research

I have actively contributed to the **GLORIA (Global Observation Research Initiative in Alpine Environments)** network, a crucial international initiative dedicated to the long-term monitoring of alpine plant diversity and the impacts of climate change on high-elevation ecosystems. My work involves establishing and meticulously re-surveying permanent plots in some of the world's most sensitive mountain environments.

Through systematic data collection on vegetation composition, species distribution, and environmental factors, I help gather crucial information on **vegetation dynamics and shifts in species distribution** due to ongoing environmental change. This data is then aggregated globally to provide insights into how alpine flora are responding to a warming climate, contributing to vital scientific assessments and conservation efforts worldwide. My field experience in diverse mountain ranges, including the Sawtooth Mountains, Lost River Range, and the Wind River Range, aligns well with GLORIA's objectives.
