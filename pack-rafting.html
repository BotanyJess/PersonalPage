What is Pack-Rafting?

Pack-rafting is a mode of travel that combines backpacking and rafting. For part of a trip the lightweight inflatable boat is carried on/in a backpack, and for part of the trip, the backpack is carried in the boat. With careful plans, pack rafting is ideal for a variety of adventure routes, particularly for crossing glacial lakes with rugged shorelines, combining river segments with hiking routes, crossing rivers too deep to ford, or landscapes with a complex mosaic of lakes and waterways. The concept of a packable lightweight boat has been around for over 150 years and became a recognized form of adventure sport beginning in the 1970s.

Owyhee River (Idaho/Oregon)

The unique geology and remoteness of the Owyhee River make it a special place indeed. I’ve returned to various segments several times over the years.

Escalante River (Utah)

Entry and exit points for the Escalante must be carefully researched. Flash floods, heat-exhaustion, and impassible routes are a real risk when planning novel routes, but add to the adventure and accomplishment of your trip!

Dirty Devil River (Utah)

Michael Kelsey's description of "The Great Alcove" on the Burr Point Trail in his book, *Non-Technical Canyon Hiking Guide to the Colorado Plateau*, was pivotal to the success of this trip.
