About Me

As a **US Marine Corps veteran** and a **botanist with a master's degree**, my life has been dedicated to both rigorous scientific exploration and adventurous outdoor pursuits. My botanical work includes a comprehensive floristic inventory of east-central Idaho, where I **discovered and published Polemonium elusum**, and extensive experience with rare plant surveys, seed collection, and spatial analysis using **ESRI's ArcPro**. I provide scientific recommendations on focal species and protocol development.

Beyond the lab and field, my passion for the wild has led me to backpack **2,000 miles of the Pacific Crest Trail**, pack raft challenging rivers like the **Colorado and Owyhee**, and explore remote regions like the **Barguzin Mountains of Siberia**. I'm also an experienced off-road driver, having navigated technical terrain and coached others in 4x4 skills. My journey is one of continuous learning and deep engagement with the natural world, understanding its climates, ecology, and geomorphology.
